Montir Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 3, 2026

Montir is a social film-ranking app: you rank the movies and shows you watch, build your lists, follow friends, and see how your taste compares with the community and with people you know. Because Montir is a social product, parts of your profile and activity are visible to other users by design. This Privacy Policy explains what information Montir ("Montir," "we," "us," or "our") collects, how we use and share it, and the choices and rights you have. This Privacy Policy is incorporated into and forms part of our Terms of Service.

2. Information we collect

We collect the following categories of information.

Information you provide

  • Phone number (required). You need a phone number to create an account. Montir uses phone-based sign-in: you enter your number and we send a one-time verification code by SMS. We do not use passwords. We use your phone number to create and secure your account and to send sign-in codes. We also store a one-way SHA-256 hash of your phone number so that other people can find you via contact matching (see Section 4). Beyond what is needed to authenticate you, we rely on this hash rather than retaining your raw number for discovery purposes.
  • Profile information. Your display name, your @handle (username), an optional bio (up to 160 characters), an optional profile photo, and your favorite genres.

Information generated by your use of Montir

  • Your activity and content. The films and shows you rank, your scores and head-to-head comparisons, your watchlist, your notes, your follows and followers, and your referrals (who invited you and whom you invited).

Information collected with your permission

  • Contacts (optional). If you grant the contacts permission, Montir reads phone numbers from your device's address book to find which of your contacts are already on Montir. As described in Section 4, these numbers are hashed on your device before they are sent to us — we never receive your contacts' names or raw phone numbers, and we do not keep a stored list of your contacts.
  • Push notification token (optional). If you enable notifications, we collect a device push token (via Expo) so we can deliver notifications to your device.

Information collected automatically

  • Technical and usage data. Standard device, app, and log data collected through our infrastructure providers — for example, app version, device type, IP address, and event logs used to operate, secure, and troubleshoot the service.

No advertising or cross-app tracking. Montir does not serve advertising, does not use third-party advertising or cross-app tracking technologies, and does not sell or share your personal information for behavioral advertising. Catalog lookups to Trakt are made server-side and contain no information that identifies you (see Section 7).

4. Contact matching and friend discovery

In short: Contact matching is optional, and it's designed so we never see your contacts' names or raw phone numbers.

If you grant Montir permission to access your contacts:

  1. The app reads the phone numbers in your device's address book.
  2. Each number is converted into a one-way SHA-256 hash on your device.
  3. Only those hashes are sent to our server, where we compare them against the hashed phone numbers of existing Montir users.
  4. Contacts who match an existing user are shown to you as people you can follow; contacts who don't match are shown as people you can invite.

We do not upload or store your contacts' names or raw phone numbers, and we do not keep the submitted hashes as a stored contact list. You can decline the contacts permission, and you can revoke it at any time in your device's operating system settings.

Being discoverable. By default, other users who have your phone number saved in their contacts may find you on Montir through the hashed-matching process above. You can turn this off using the "let contacts find me" setting, which turns off your discoverability so you are no longer surfaced to others through contact matching.

5. What other users can see

In short: Montir is a social app, so your profile and activity are visible to others.

The following are visible to other Montir users:

  • Your profile — display name, @handle, bio, profile photo, and favorite genres.
  • Your rankings and scores.
  • Your activity on the app.

Some figures — such as community or friends' average scores — may be gated behind inviting friends. That is a product feature designed to encourage participation; it is not a privacy control and does not change what others can see about you.

If you don't want certain information to be visible, don't add it to your profile, or delete your account (see Section 9).

If you enable notifications, we collect a device push token (via Expo) and use it — together with APNs (Apple) or FCM (Google) — to send you notifications such as a new follower or a friend joining from your invite. You can turn off push notifications at any time in your device's operating system settings; doing so does not affect your ability to use the rest of the app.

9. Data retention and deleting your account

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the service, and afterward only as required to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce our agreements. By data category:

  • Account, profile, and activity data. Retained while your account is active. When you delete your account via Profile → Account → Delete account, this data is cascade-deleted from our live database.
  • Phone-number hash used for discovery. Retained while your account exists; turning off the "let contacts find me" setting stops you from being surfaced through contact matching, and the hash is removed when you delete your account.
  • Submitted contact hashes (from contact matching). Not retained — they are used transiently to match against existing users and are not stored as a contact list (see Section 4).
  • SMS verification. Handled by Twilio Verify and not stored by us beyond what authentication requires.
  • Backups. Residual copies may remain in our routine backups for a limited period before being overwritten on our normal backup cycle.
  • Legal holds. We may retain limited records longer where we are required to do so by law or for legitimate purposes such as fraud prevention.

Deleting your account. You can delete your account directly in the app at Profile → Account → Delete account. You can also request deletion by emailing us at MontirTechnologies@gmail.com.
